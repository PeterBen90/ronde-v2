The duration of your renovation depends on the type of project and your specific needs. For example, a bathroom renovation may take a few weeks, while full home or commercial renovations can span several months. Factors like project complexity, custom design choices, material availability, and your schedule preferences all play a role. During the planning phase, we work closely with you to establish a clear timeline and keep you informed every step of the way.

YBefore starting any major renovation or construction in Ontario, you’ll likely need one or more permits to ensure the work meets safety, zoning, and building‑code standards. The most common is a building permit, which is required for things like structural changes (adding rooms, removing load-bearing walls), finishing basements, plumbing, electrical or mechanical system modifications, new additions, or when changing the use of a building.

Municipal zoning laws must also be respected, and in some cases a minor variance or special approval could be needed if your plans don’t comply (for example, with setbacks, lot coverage, or heritage designations).

Skipping the required permits can lead to serious consequences, including fines, stop‑work orders, having to undo completed work, or issues when selling or insuring the property.

Whether you can stay in your home during renovations depends on the size and type of the project. For smaller updates like painting or flooring, living in your home is usually no problem. However, for major renovations involving plumbing, electrical work, or structural changes, it may be safer and more comfortable to temporarily relocate until critical phases are complete. We’ll work with you to plan the renovation schedule and minimize disruption, keeping your comfort and safety a top priority throughout the process.
